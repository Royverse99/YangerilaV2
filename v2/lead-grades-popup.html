Lead Guitar Playing Technique

Melodies, solos, and expression come alive

Lead guitar is where melodies, solos, and expression come alive. It’s the voice of the guitar, from blues licks to shredding electric solos. While rhythm provides the backbone, lead adds color and character, letting you stand out as a guitarist. Our Lead Grades are designed to take you step by step from playing simple melodies to performing confident solos.

About the Course:

Yangerila’s Lead Grade 1 is designed to create a strong foundation for your lead guitar journey. With complete control over major and minor scales, students learn not just to cover their favorite solos and riffs, but also to improvise from the very early stage. Techniques such as the CAGED system, sweep picking, tapping, and alternate picking help students explore, experiment, and eventually find their own sound.

What You’ll Learn:

  • Lead guitar techniques (Alternate picking, tapping etc)
  • Major and minor scales across the fretboard
  • The CAGED system for lead movement
  • Other techniques: Bending, sliding, vibrato, hammer-ons & pull-offs
  • Intro to Advanced techniques: Sweep picking and tapping
  • Building simple solos from scale patterns
  • Application of power chords
  • Famous riffs and solos
  • Introduction to improvisation

Upon Completion:

By the end of Lead Grade 1, students will be able to confidently play their favorite songs, riffs and solos. They will have a strong understanding of Major and minor scales all over the fret board, which will enable them to play fillers between rhythm sections, and improvise short passages using scales. They’ll have a strong technical base with control over core techniques, making them ready to tackle more advanced lead playing and explore their own style.

About the Course:

Yangerila’s Lead Grade 2 takes your lead guitar playing beyond the basics, focusing on expressive soloing, improvisation, and stylistic mastery. While Grade 1 builds your technical base, Grade 2 is where you start sounding like a true lead guitarist—confident, fluid, and unique. Students dive deeper into phrasing, speed building, and improvisation, while exploring how different genres approach lead guitar.

Prerequisite:

To apply for this course, you must have completed Yangerila’s Lead Grade 1. If you’ve successfully completed Grade 1, please contact your Student Relations Manager (SRM) or your teacher to apply for this grade.
